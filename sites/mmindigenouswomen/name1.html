Purpose of the MMIWG & Violence Prevention Policy The Native Women's Association of Canada has been working diligently towards resolving the senseless acts of violence affecting women, girls, and gender-diverse within Indigenous communities. The main purpose of their work is to provide healing and support services with long-term funding; these services are available for the community and family of these missing and murdered women, girls, and gender-diverse. By completing this, the NWAC hopes to create a community based around building strength and resilience ("Vision Statement"), as well as empower the indigenous community. They have created at least four educational and awareness based 'toolkits' for indigenous peoples to utilize. The 'toolkits' discussed below are: Honouring Indigenous Women, You Are Not Alone, Sisters in Spirit, and the Faceless Doll Project. Honouring Indigenous Women Indigenous communities, specifically the traditional roles individuals take on, were deeply affected by colonizers. With the Honouring Indigenous Women toolkit, the NWAC is attempting to give Indigenous women, girls, and gender-diverse the means to reclaim their heritage, as well as reinstate honor, empowerment, and respect to Native American culture and community. Furthermore, the NWAC hope to provide insight into a better understanding of the roles their ancestors took on. By doing so, they are able to give Indigenous youth a safe platform, where they can strengthen their understanding of traditional Indigenous values and heritage. Their focus for Indigenous youth resides in "traditional roles on women, defining respect and honor, valuing one's self, mindfulness, sustainability in culture, 2SLGBTQQIA, MMIWG and more" ("Honouring Indigenous Women"). This prepares these young children to think critically about future issues that may arise in Indigenous communities, and any personal adversities they may face. It brings them closer to their community and ancestry, as well. You Are Not Alone Indigenous women, girls, and gender-diverse are frequently confronted with the harsh reality of a settler colonial society. Years of discrimination, sexism, and racism have led to higher violence rates towards Indigenous women, than non-Indigenous women; the violence encountered commonly takes place within Indigenous families and communities. Indigenous women who are trapped in an abusive relationship will often lack the necessary resources to escape. The NWAC is providing victims of domestic violence with a safely planning toolkit to address their personal situation, as well as various educational resources (such as background information on how colonization has affected native communities, topics relating to domestic violence, support systems available within their community, and strategies for leaving an abuser.) Their goal is to support these women, and help them to realize they have the ability to change their environment for the better. The You Are Not Alone toolkit is helping to slowly eliminate the level of violence found in indigenous communities; it creates a healthy, safe, and happy environment for future generations to thrive.
